Dylan Ricardo

I started programming at the age of 17 and because I got passionate about the area I started my degree in Computer Science. I chose to study at PUCPR because it is a renowned college and have received a scholarship due to my performance. I was developing an open ledger, in blockchain format to study the Python language when I had the opportunity to work at Turing. During this experience, I improved my knowledge in Python and developed in other areas such as front-end and database. Thanks to the work dynamics of the Turing team, several projects went through me and I was able, then, to have a very deep contact with all areas, opening up a range of skills that I have developed with the objective of leading projects with confidence and insight into what needs to be done.

The language I am most familiar with is Python, followed by JavaScript. HTML and CSS were new to me at the beginning of Turing, however, I have had the necessary practice to safely do multiple screens alone. On the database side, I worked with MongoDB at my part-time job and executed several SQL projects in college, being identical my level of knowledge and skill in both tools. The frameworks with which I had the most contact were Flask and JQuery, however I have developed solo projects with Node.js, React and React-Native.

Turing Technology

Financial Consulting

Part-time jobs as a Junior Developer from 2019 to August 2020.

Turing is a start-up in the financial technology area that aims to provide the investor with information about their investments. With tools that simulate your portfolio, compare investments and provide performance rankings, Turing has the objective to make the investment world transparent so that every investor and market professional has the necessary information to guide their decisions.

Working at Turing I was able to develop practical knowledge that I had only seen in theory before. Using Python on the back-end and Python-Flask for the routes I was able to go deeper into language, in addition to having challenges that no course can provide. HTML and CSS were used for building the front-end and, to give life to the tool, vanilla JavaScript was used and the JQuery library / framework. I was able to have experience with the MongoDB database and practice incessantly Linux.

I had the honorous experience of having Rodrigo Passerino and Lucca Portes as my mentors for the art of programming, Roberta Samistraro as a coworker, in addition to André Chede and Mehanna Mehanna as mentors in the area of ​​ investments and personal finance, who also taught me a lot about the day-to-day commercial area of ​​a fintech / start-up.

devradar

Application developed during the event: Omnistack Week 10.

The application aimed to connect developers with the necessary knowledge based on their location.
The user registered and using the github API the user's bio and photo were shown in addition to the technologies registered by the user, in order to locate developers with the skills you are looking for. The most interesting part of this project was certainly the use of the developer's latitude and longitude to fill in a map in real time each user with a preview of their information. Doing this not only aimed to show developers closer physically to those looking for them, but also to use the Google map in an application.
